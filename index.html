Fruits and Their Scientific Names


Fruits are natural sources of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making them an essential part of a balanced diet. They come in a wide variety of shapes, flavors, and textures, each with its own unique characteristics. Understanding their scientific names helps highlight the diversity of plant species we consume daily. Below is a list of different fruits with their scientific names and a brief description of each.



Banana

(Musa acuminata)

Bananas are soft, sweet fruits encased in a yellow peel, widely known for their potassium content.

Apple

(Malus domestica)

Apples are crisp, sweet or tart fruits commonly eaten fresh or used in cooking and baking.

Orange

(Citrus × sinensis)

Oranges are citrus fruits valued for their refreshing juice and high vitamin C content.

Pineapple

(Ananas comosus)

Pineapples are tropical fruits with spiky skin and sweet, tangy flesh used in both savory and sweet dishes.

Mango

(Mangifera indica)

Mangoes are juicy, tropical fruits with a sweet, rich flavor and a large central pit.

Strawberry

(Fragaria × ananassa)

Strawberries are bright red berries known for their juicy texture and sweet-tart flavor.

Watermelon

(Citrullus lanatus)

Watermelons are large, juicy fruits with a green rind and red, water-rich flesh that’s perfect for hot days.

Grape

(Vitis vinifera)

Grapes are small, sweet or tart fruits eaten fresh, dried as raisins, or fermented into wine.

Papaya

(Carica papaya)

Papayas are tropical fruits with orange flesh and black seeds, often enjoyed for their digestive benefits.

Avocado

(Persea americana)

Avocados are creamy-textured fruits rich in healthy fats, often used in salads, spreads, or smoothies.
